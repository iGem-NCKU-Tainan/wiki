Safety
Safety Issues
How we are addressing any safety issues in our project?
  • Choosing a non-pathogenic chassis: E. coli DH5α, E. coli BL21 (DE3)
  • In our product, the chassis is in small enclosed space as a barrier to prevent contamination.
  • In the enclosed space, there are “suicide-gene” inducers to kill E. coli in the bio-sensing strip.
Safety Plan

Our E. coli will sensor urea glucose. When the glucose level is high, the bacteria can express red fluorescent protein (RFP) as reporter to give an signal. The signal from fluorescence can be detected using excitation. After the processing, the “suicide-gene” inducers, arabinose, will induce the pBAD promoter (BBa_K206000) and then activate the transcription of Lysis protein (BBa_K117000).

Safe Lab
We are working in safe lab

We operate the project in the Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) Lab. The space is suit for experiment in all of our chassis. They are Risk Group 1 organisms. In the experiment, we always handle biological materials in laminar flow hood. All of biowaste are autoclaved at the typical 121 °C/15 psi for 15 minutes.

Laws and Regulations
We follow the laws and regulations of biosafety in our Country/ University

"The Environmental Protection, Safety and Health Center" is responsible for the safety of biology labs at Nation Cheng-Kung University (Guidelines for Laboratory Biosafety). And, "Centers for Disease Control" is the department in our country that govern biosafety in research laboratories (Laboratory Biosafety Management: A Compilation of Regulations and Administrative Guidance).
